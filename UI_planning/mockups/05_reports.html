Executive Summary

This grounded theory analysis of three in-depth interviews with RAND Corporation researchers reveals a complex landscape of AI adoption in qualitative research methodologies. The study identifies AI Adoption and Integration in Research as the core phenomenon, with researchers navigating between opportunities for efficiency gains and concerns about methodological rigor.

The analysis uncovered 24 distinct codes organized into 5 major categories, revealing that researchers primarily view AI as a tool for enhancing rather than replacing traditional research methods. Key applications include code generation, qualitative data analysis, and proposal writing, while significant concerns center on ethical implications and technical limitations.

Key Findings

FINDING 1
AI as Efficiency Enabler
Researchers consistently report that AI tools significantly reduce time spent on routine tasks, particularly in coding and initial data analysis, allowing more focus on interpretation and theory development.
FINDING 2
Methodological Tensions
A clear tension exists between the desire for AI-enhanced efficiency and concerns about maintaining methodological rigor, with researchers emphasizing the need for human oversight and validation.
FINDING 3
Organizational Readiness Gap
While individual researchers show enthusiasm for AI tools, organizational infrastructure and training lag behind, creating barriers to systematic adoption across research teams.
